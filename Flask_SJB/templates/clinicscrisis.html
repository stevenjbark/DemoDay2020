COVID-19 Outbreak

The critical importance of health clinics in the healthcare infrastructure of the United States has become clear during the infection outbreak of the Novel Coronavirus or COVID-19. The data presented below are for the total number of daily cases of COVID-19 and the daily number of deaths attributed to COVID-19, both from January 22 to April 14, 2020.

As demonstrated in the maps above, many outbreak regions are in population centers with particular focus in areas of moderate-to-lower income. These areas often will not have major hospital infrastructure capable of handling large-scale outbreaks of infectious disease. However, even in places where extensive hospital capacity is available (for example New York City), the COVID-19 pandemic has strained the healthcare infrastructure often to the breaking point.

Another important observation is the expanded number of infections observed in the Midwest and SouthEastern United States, often in predominantly rural counties or counties surrounding the major population centers. These counties do not have the major healthcare infrastructure required to combat a pandemic-level infection outbreak. As such, the local and regional health clinics play a critical role in protecting the populations they serve and the entire health infrastructure of the United States.
